Our Story

Kobo's History

From a Harvard research project to the world's most widely used humanitarian data platform — two decades of impact.

The Beginning

Born from a Need to Better Understand Conflict

Kobo's story begins in 2005, when researchers Phuong Pham and Patrick Vinck at Harvard's Humanitarian Initiative (HHI) set out to solve a critical problem: how to collect reliable, structured data in conflict and post-conflict settings.

The tools available at the time were either too expensive, too complex, or not suited to the realities of field work in challenging environments. So they built their own.

That tool — which would become KoboToolbox — was designed from the ground up to be accessible, reliable offline, and free for the organizations that needed it most. The name "Kobo" comes from the Acholi word meaning "transfer" — a reflection of the mission to transfer data capability to communities everywhere.

Key Milestones

Two Decades of Impact

From a small research tool to a global platform trusted by the world's leading humanitarian organizations.

2005

Kobo Founded at Harvard HHI

Phuong Pham and Patrick Vinck founded Kobo at the Harvard Humanitarian Initiative (HHI), creating the first version of what would become KoboToolbox — a tool to improve data collection in conflict and post-conflict settings.

2013–2014

Expansion to a Global Platform

Through partnerships with OCHA (the UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs) and IRC (the International Rescue Committee), KoboToolbox expanded dramatically — becoming a truly global platform for humanitarian data collection.

2019

Kobo Established as Independent Nonprofit

Kobo was established as an independent nonprofit organization, registered in Cambridge, Massachusetts. This milestone allowed Kobo to operate with greater autonomy and focus purely on its mission to provide accessible data technology for social good.

2023

OCHA Humanitarian Server Transferred to Kobo

Ownership and operation of the OCHA humanitarian server was formally transferred to Kobo — a significant step in consolidating Kobo's role as the primary steward of KoboToolbox for the global humanitarian community. Kobo also launched its first KoboToolbox Academy Course.

2024

Ambassadors Program & Kobo Partner Services Launched

Kobo launched two major new initiatives: the KoboToolbox Ambassadors Program — a global volunteer network supporting data use in local communities — and Kobo Partner Services, providing expert data support directly to nonprofits.

2025

Kobo Research Officially Launched

Kobo officially launched Kobo Research — its fourth core initiative — to support organizations in using data for evaluation, planning, and advocacy through applied research partnerships. This marks Kobo's continued evolution as a full-service data organization for social good.

Where We Are Today

A Global Leader in Humanitarian Data Technology

Today, Kobo is a global team working across dozens of countries, serving over 32,000 nonprofits with free, open-source tools — and pushing the frontiers of data innovation for positive social change.
